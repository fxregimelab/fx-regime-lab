About the author

Shreyash Sakhare

I am an electrical engineering graduate (B.Tech, Pune) building toward a career in quantamental macro—the overlap between disciplined, data-backed signals and human judgment about regimes, policy, and risk. FX Regime Lab is my public research stack: a daily pipeline that ingests public market data, merges it into one dated dataset, and publishes regime reads with transparent validation—not as a product pitch, but as a living notebook anyone can inspect and challenge.

Mission

The mission is simple: institutional-grade discipline with zero gatekeeping. Major dollar pairs and INR, rates, positioning, volatility, and cross-asset context—documented sources, logged calls, rolling accuracy. If the framework is wrong, the record should show it. If it is useful, it should be reproducible from the same public inputs.

What ships on this site

The terminal is the interactive surface for depth; the brief and AI article view are narrative exports for a linear read; the dashboard is the high-level live snapshot. Together they are research and education only—not investment advice, not a solicitation, and not a promise of performance.

For how signals are built and ordered, read the methodology. For notes and essays, see the newsletter and Substack.
