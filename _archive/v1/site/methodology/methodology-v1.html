Methodology

How the daily pipeline is built

FX Regime Lab ingests public market data, merges it into a single daily dataset, and publishes regime labels with rolling validation. Nothing here is investment advice; it is a transparent research stack you can reproduce from documented sources.

Pipeline order

The orchestrated sequence is: FX and yield ETL, CFTC COT positioning, INR-specific metrics, optional volatility and open-interest layers, risk-reversal inputs where configured, merge into a master table, text brief, optional macro helpers, AI-assisted narrative (when enabled), HTML brief with charts, then deploy. Failed upstream fetches are isolated so the rest of the run can complete.

Signal families

  • Rates and spreads — policy and benchmark yield differentials that anchor G10 FX.
  • Positioning — CFTC Commitments of Traders for liquid futures proxies.
  • Volatility and skew — realized and implied vol, plus optionality metrics where available.
  • Cross-asset — DXY, oil, equity vol, and related macro series as context, not standalone alpha.
  • Regime call — a structured label with confidence and primary driver, logged for next-day checks.

Validation

Calls are compared to realized price behaviour on a defined horizon; rolling accuracy appears in the terminal and brief headers. Gaps or source errors are logged for monitoring.

A formal paper-style write-up is planned alongside the public site. Until then, the morning brief and terminal charts remain the live specification of what shipped each day.
